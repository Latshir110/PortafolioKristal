About Me

I am a video game programmer with a strong interest in innovation and the development of interactive experiences. My main focus has been to integrate the physical world with the digital world through video games, developing solutions that connect technology with people in meaningful ways.

Professional Experience

Applied Video Game Development Researcher | Universidad Bernardo O’Higgins

I worked in a research internship focused on the development and testing of a video game prototype, conducting research on sensors applicable to the project and verifying its proper functionality in conjunction with a haptic vest, as part of a collaboration with University College of London (UCL).

My Projects

Video Game with Electromyogram

EMG SHOOTER is an innovative project designed to provide accessibility for people with manual disabilities. The game is controlled through arm movement intention, using biological signals to detect and translate motion into gameplay actions.

Presentation of my research project

I was accepted to present my research on EEG in Video Games as a pitch at the R&D seedbed through Gamification, organized by Sergio Covarrubias at Bernardo O'Higgins University.
